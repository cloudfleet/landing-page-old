cloud|fleet - be the captain of your data



What is cloud|fleet?

A box that makes it easy to host the essential internet services at home.

It will start with basic features like mail, file synchronization and note taking but we will expand the list as fast as possible.

We have enough ideas and plans but would also welcome your suggestions.

3 steps to heaven

  1. Select your domain
  2. Order cloud fleet box
  3. Plug into router

 

Now you can send mails, take notes, store files from all your devices without relying on 3rd party services like Google, Evernote or Dropbox.

Features

Mail

The mail client included is provided by the mailpile project, that aims to create a mail client that is focuses on search, privacy and encryption

File Synchronization

For file synchronization cloud|fleet includes ownCloud, an open source project that makes your data available from everywhere.

Note taking

To write and access your notes from anywhere, cloud|fleet includes a customized version of OpenNote

Contact Management

For contact management cloud|fleet includes ownCloud, an open source project that makes your data available from everywhere.

Calendar

For calendar synchronization cloud|fleet includes ownCloud, an open source project that makes your data available from everywhere.

Backup

With the box you will also get backup space. This backup will be encrypted on your box before it is sent to us. So in anything goes wrong, you will always be able to restore your data.

FAQ

why should I use cloud|fleet?

Because with cloud|fleet you can have all the convenience of a cloud service while retaining your privacy

Because your data should be yours. Don't let the NSA or Google snoop on you.

The cloud|fleet box with your data is at your home. That means to get to your data people can't simply give a secret order to Google to turn it over.

Because cloud|fleet can grow with your needs. When you need more space, you can just attach an external harddrive.

can I try out cloud|fleet?

You will be able to try out cloud|fleet on a box that is located with us for a week. If you like it you will be able to transfer your data to the device.

will you use free software?

Most of the software will be licensed under the AGPL license. We will not use any closed source software.
Everything we develop on our own will be published on github.

will cloud fleet run on X?

While we will not give any guarantees everything should be adaptable for your system.
We will be happy to help people out with any porting endeavours.

Can I share the box?

Yes. But we recommend to have your own. The administrator of the box will always have access to all the data in the box. So if you really want to be the captain of your own data, get your box.

how will you make money?

  • We will get off the ground with a crowd funding campaign
  • We will sell readily imaged devices
  • We will also charge a monthly fee for the firewall tunnelling service and the regular security and feature updates.
  • We will offer additional services like backup and insurance in case your box breaks down

Motivation

1. Privacy

Recent developments showed that big providers cannot be trusted to be able to keep your data secure, even if they have the best intentions to do no evil.

2. Accessability

Big providers can shut you out of your account for no reason whatsoever, taking your data with them. Only by reclaiming the cloud can you guarantee access to your own data forever.

3. Resilience

With a big network of inexpensive boxes, no single outtage can have a devastating effect like the shutdown of a big provider

4. Expandability

If you own your data you can augment your cloud with additional as they get available.

Architecture

Hardware

The cloud|fleet stack will run on any hardware. It will be optimized for the CuBox Pro of SolidRun.

Software

Base System

The base system will be a hardened version of Ubuntu that exposes a minimal attack interface.

Firewall Tunneling

To make deployment behind firewalls possible, the cloud|fleet box will connect to a pagekite server.
Later it will be possible for nodes with a public ip to act as pagekite providers themselves to decentralize this component.

A separate system will be used for exposing the mail server.
